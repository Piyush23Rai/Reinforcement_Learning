üß† Neural RL: Two Different Approaches

DQN (Q-Learning) vs PPO (Policy Gradient) - Understanding How Neural Networks Learn Decisions

üéØ The Two Approaches
üéÆ Option A: DQN (Deep Q-Network)
What it learns:

Q-values = Long-term profit scores for each action

How it decides:

Pick the highest score: argmax Q(state, action)

If Q(SEND)=222 and Q(NO)=221, pick SEND ‚úì

Real-world analogy:

üí∞ A score card that grades each action: "SEND scores 222/10, NO scores 221/10"

You always pick the highest score.

üé≤ Option B: PPO (Proximal Policy Optimization)
What it learns:

Probabilities = Chance to take each action

How it decides:

Roll the dice: P(SEND)=0.94, P(NO)=0.06

Usually picks SEND (94%), but sometimes tries NO (6%) üé≤

Real-world analogy:

üé∞ A probability distribution: "94% of the time do SEND, 6% of the time do NO"

You act randomly according to these odds.

üí° Key Difference:

DQN: "Here are the scores, always pick the highest" (Deterministic)

PPO: "Here are the odds, roll the dice" (Stochastic)

This means: If the network learned about "LOYAL customers", it can also make smart decisions about "VERY_LOYAL customers" - similar states!

ü§î When to Use DQN vs PPO?
üéÆ Use DQN When:

‚úì You have discrete action spaces (SEND or NO, not infinite choices)

‚úì You need deterministic decisions ("always pick SEND")

‚úì You want simple implementation

‚úì Problem: Can be unstable during training üòï

üé≤ Use PPO When:

‚úì You have continuous or discrete actions

‚úì You want stable training

‚úì You want natural exploration built-in

‚úì Benefit: Much more stable than DQN! ‚úÖ

üìö Summary for Your Classroom

üéì The Big Picture:

Tabular RL: "I have a table that shows the profit for EACH state-action pair"

Neural RL (DQN): "I have a neural network that learns to score actions - like a grading system"

Neural RL (PPO): "I have a neural network that learns to assign probabilities to actions - like a probability distribution"

‚ö° Why Neural Networks?

With 1000 possible states, a table would be HUGE. But a neural network with just 128 hidden neurons can learn smart policies that generalize to all states!

üöÄ Why This Matters:

PPO is more stable and is used in cutting-edge applications like ChatGPT training (RLHF). DQN is simpler but trickier to train.

üé¨ Live Example: Different Scenarios

Scenario 1: A New Customer

DQN says: "New customers look like PRICE-SENSITIVE customers. Q(SEND)=187.57, Q(NO)=187.16. Barely better to SEND."

PPO says: "New customers look like PRICE-SENSITIVE customers. P(SEND)=76.7%. Send, but with some doubt."

Scenario 2: Competitor Sends Free Coupons

DQN problem: Q-values might diverge (become very large numbers). Network becomes unstable.

PPO advantage: Probabilities stay between 0-1. Clipping prevents huge swings. More robust!

Scenario 3: Testing New Strategy

DQN: Uses Œµ-greedy exploration. As Œµ decreases from 0.56 ‚Üí 0.05, less likely to try new things.

PPO: Natural entropy keeps exploration alive. Even final policy has some randomness (94% vs 6%).
